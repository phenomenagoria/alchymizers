

---

Theme Design Document

Game Title

“Alchymizers: Backcountry Moonshine Distillers”

Premise:

Players are backcountry distillers in the Appalachian hills, competing to produce the finest and most profitable moonshine batch while avoiding bad mash blowouts and sheriff attention.

Each round represents one distilling run.


---

Core Narrative

Every player owns a hidden still deep in the hills.

During a run they:

1. Pull ingredients blindly from their stash sack


2. Throw them into the copper still


3. The mixture flows through a long copper coil run



If too much bad mash ends up in the run, pressure builds and the still blows out.

The further the batch travels through the coil, the stronger and more valuable the liquor.


---

Visual Design

The Player Board

Instead of a potion pot, players have a moonshine still setup.

The track is a coiled copper pipe that winds outward from the still.

Layout Concept

Center: A large copper still pot sitting on a stone furnace.

The spiral track represents:

The copper condenser coil where the distillate travels.

As ingredients are added, the alcohol runs further down the coil.

The farther it travels, the higher the proof and the higher the price.


---

Track Space Theme

Each space on the coil pipe has two icons:

Dollars

Represents selling the batch locally.

Icon:
A glass mason jar with dollar bills inside

Reputation

Represents your reputation as the best distiller in the holler.

Icon:
A blue ribbon tied to a mason jar


---

Special Track Spaces

Copper Bit (Ruby equivalent)

Icon: A small copper rivet or scrap metal piece

Lore: Distillers salvage spare copper from equipment to upgrade their still.

Effect: Collecting copper lets you extend the coil start point.


---

Start Line Marker (Droplet equivalent)

Icon: A small flame under the still furnace

Lore: Upgrading the still means the batch starts further down the coil.

Visually: A little fire icon moves forward on the track.


---

Stash Bag Theme

Instead of a bag of potion ingredients:

Players draw from a canvas feed sack.

Icon idea: A burlap sack labeled “MASH SUPPLY”

Narrative: It contains random mash components and additives you toss into the still.


---

Ingredient Tokens (Highly Detailed)

Tokens are wooden mash pucks or ingredient tiles.

Each ingredient has:

icon

color

flavor explanation

mechanical ability



---

1. Bad Mash (Explosion tokens)

Color: Dirty white / grey

Icon: Rotting corn mash with bubbles

Narrative:

Bad mash means contamination — wild bacteria, rotten grain, or mold.

Too much of it causes the still pressure to spike.

If the mash goes bad, the still blows its lid.

Visual idea:

A barrel of spoiled mash with flies.

Token text:

Bad Mash
Values: 1 / 2 / 3


---

2. Corn (Basic filler)

Color: Orange

Icon: Corn cob

Narrative:

Corn is the base ingredient for most Appalachian moonshine.

It's reliable and cheap but not very powerful.

Corn helps the mash move through the still without adding risk.

Ability:

None.


---

3. Juniper Berries

Color: Green

Icon: Juniper branch with berries

Narrative:

Some distillers add herbs to improve flavor.

Juniper adds clarity and aroma, which helps salvage extra copper scraps.

Ability theme:

Good flavoring means you can salvage copper bits from the equipment.

Flavor text:

“Clean run, good smell, copper stays shiny.”


---

4. Charcoal Filter

Color: Dark red

Icon: Charcoal chunks or a filter barrel

Narrative:

Charcoal filtering removes impurities.

The more corn mash already in the still, the better the filter works.

Ability theme:

Filtering improves the batch strength dramatically.

Mechanically:

Boost based on corn already added.


---

5. Copper Coil Upgrade

Color: Blue

Icon: Coiled copper tubing

Narrative:

Better coil design lets you test the vapor early.

Distillers sometimes sniff the early vapor to see if the run is good.

Ability theme:

Test the next ingredient safely.

If it’s bad mash, discard it.


---

6. Sugar

Color: Yellow

Icon: Sugar cube pile

Narrative:

Sugar increases fermentation strength.

But the more sugar you add, the stronger the effect.

Ability theme:

Sugar compounds with itself.


---

7. Ghost Pepper

Color: Purple

Icon: A glowing pepper

Narrative:

Some insane distillers add hot peppers to give their liquor a reputation.

Too much burns like hell but becomes legendary.

Ability theme:

End-of-round prestige bonuses.


---

Event Cards (Holler Cards)

Instead of fortune telling cards:

Players draw Holler Events.

These represent things happening in the backwoods that night.

Examples:


---

Sheriff’s Patrol

“The sheriff is riding through the valley tonight.”

Effect: Everyone starts cautiously.

First bad mash counts +1.


---

Cold Mountain Spring

“Pure spring water flows down the ridge.”

Effect: Everyone gains 1 copper.


---

Bootleg Demand

“The town dance is tonight.”

Effect: Extra money from your batch.


---

Rival Distiller

“Someone else in the holler is stepping up their game.”

Effect: Leader gets small penalty.


---

Rubies → Copper Bits

Copper is extremely valuable for still construction.

Visual:

Small copper scrap pieces.

Players spend them to:

Upgrade the still.


---

Blowout (Explosion)

When a still blows:

Visual concept:

The lid flies off the still and mash sprays everywhere.

Sound concept (if she adds audio):

Steam hiss + metal clang

Narrative:

The run is ruined.

You must choose:

save your reputation
or

salvage whatever liquor you can sell.



---

Market (Ingredient buying)

Instead of a potion shop:

Players visit the Supply Wagon.

Visual:

A wooden wagon filled with barrels and sacks.

Vendor idea:

An old mountain trader named Old Maggie.


---

Currency

Coins become dollars.

Art idea:

Stacks of folded bills or mason jars of cash.


---

Endgame Narrative

After nine distilling runs, the holler decides:

Who is the Master Distiller of the Mountains.

Winner is the one with the most reputation.
